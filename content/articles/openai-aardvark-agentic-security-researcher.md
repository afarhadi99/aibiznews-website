## Introduction

OpenAI announced Aardvark on Oct 30, 2025: an agentic, GPT-5-powered security researcher designed to scale defensive workflows with human oversight.

OpenAI just unveiled Aardvark, an agentic security researcher now in private beta. Built on GPT-5, Aardvark runs end-to-end workflows that look a lot like what real security engineers do-from triaging issues and generating targeted tests, to validating findings and drafting reports. The goal isn't point-and-click exploits; it's systematic, auditable research at scale. Teams can feed it codebases or environments, then supervise steps as Aardvark explains its reasoning and evidence. OpenAI says it's inviting partners to benchmark accuracy and reduce false positives across diverse stacks. If it works, this could compress weeks of analysis into hours, elevate coverage, and standardize best practices. It also raises the bar for defensive AI-using advanced models to proactively probe systems before attackers do. Expect early pilots to focus on CI pipelines and high-risk surfaces, with careful human oversight. Bottom line: Aardvark points to a new class of AI coworkers that do complex, multi-step security work-explainably, at scale, and under policy controls.
