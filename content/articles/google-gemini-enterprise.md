## Introduction

Google's Gemini Enterprise is a secure platform for deploying AI agents across everyday workflows, launched October 9, 2025. It combines Gemini models, no-code agent building, enterprise integrations, and governance features like Model Armor - aiming to move companies from pilots to production.

Google just turned AI agents into a product line. Gemini Enterprise, announced October 9, 2025, is the front door for workplace AI: a secure platform where teams deploy prebuilt and custom agents, integrate enterprise data, and automate real workflows - without writing code.

Think less chatbot, more orchestration. Agents search across Drive and third-party apps, summarize, draft, and take actions. IT gets governance, Model Armor for safety, and centralized controls; employees get speed. Pricing starts per user per month, aimed squarely at broad adoption.

Under the hood, Gemini models power multimodal understanding and tool use. Add Model Context Protocol to connect systems and you have agents that can reason over docs, spreadsheets, tickets, and dashboards - then actually do things.

This isn't a demo reel. Partners are shipping integrations, and release notes show rapid updates. The direction is clear: AI that fits into work, not the other way around.

For enterprises stalled at pilots, Gemini Enterprise is Google's bid to make agents practical at scale.
