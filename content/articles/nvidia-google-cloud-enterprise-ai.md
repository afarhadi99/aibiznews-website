Google Cloud announced the general availability of G4 virtual machines equipped with NVIDIA RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell Server Edition GPUs. Alongside the VMs, NVIDIA Omniverse and NVIDIA Isaac Sim are now offered as virtual machine images on Google Cloud Marketplace, making it easier for teams to build digital twins, run physics-based robotics simulations, and accelerate design and visualization workflows.

## What's in the update

- G4 instances can scale to eight RTX PRO 6000 GPUs (up to 768 GB of GDDR7) for high-performance inference and visual computing.
- Integrations with Google Kubernetes Engine and Vertex AI simplify containerized deployments and MLOps.
- Support for data analytics stacks like Dataproc helps connect ETL, training, and inference in one pipeline.

## Why it matters

This release focuses on practical adoption. Rather than speculative roadmaps, enterprises get a deployable option to speed up real workloads-multimodal assistants, computer-aided engineering, creative pipelines, and simulation-on infrastructure that can be provisioned on demand.

## Use cases in focus

- Digital twins for factories and products using Omniverse and OpenUSD.
- Robotics development with Isaac Sim before real-world rollout.
- Remote creative and design pipelines using RTX Virtual Workstation capabilities.

## Context

The update aligns with the broader trend of pairing accelerated computing with agentic and physical AI. By packaging GPUs and software as managed cloud offerings, teams can test, iterate, and deploy faster while maintaining predictable operations.
