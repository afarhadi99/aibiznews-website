## Introduction

NVIDIA and SK Group announced a 50,000+ GPU AI factory in Korea on Oct 31, 2025 to accelerate chip R&D, digital twins, robotics, and AI agents.

NVIDIA and SK Group are building a national-scale AI factory in Korea with more than 50,000 GPUs. Announced Oct 31 at APEC, the facility targets chip design, digital twins, robotics, and AI agent development-with cloud access for enterprises and research. Think of it as industrial AI infrastructure: standardized stacks, shared acceleration, and direct pipelines into Omniverse, NIM, and NeMo. For Korea's manufacturing base, this means faster simulation cycles and smarter automation; for startups, on-demand compute to train and deploy. If timelines hold, pilots could spin up quickly with partner workloads migrating first. Strategically, it's another signal that AI capacity is becoming a national asset-and that next-gen factories will be co-designed by software, silicon, and sovereign policy. Watch for export-friendly model hosting, robotics sandboxes, and digital-twin toolchains to go live in phases.
